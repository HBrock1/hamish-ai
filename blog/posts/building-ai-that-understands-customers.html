Building AI That Actually Understands Customers

Originally published on Substack

Most customer analytics tools are built backwards. They start with the data and try to force insights out of it. Charts, dashboards, endless pivots — all in service of answering a question nobody asked.

When I started building Brox.AI, I wanted to flip that model on its head. Instead of starting with data, we start with questions. Real questions that product managers, customer success teams, and executives actually have.

The Problem with Traditional Analytics

Here's what typically happens: A company invests in a "customer 360" platform. They spend months integrating data sources. They hire analysts to build dashboards. And then... nothing changes.

The dashboards sit there, slowly becoming outdated. The insights they promised remain buried under layers of complexity. Meanwhile, actual decisions get made on gut feel and anecdote.

The best analytics tools don't show you more data. They show you less — but the right less.

A Different Approach

At Brox.AI, we built something different. Instead of forcing users to learn SQL or navigate complex dashboards, we let them ask questions in plain English. The AI does the heavy lifting — understanding intent, querying the right data, and surfacing insights that actually matter.

This isn't just a chatbot slapped onto a database. It's a fundamentally different way of thinking about customer intelligence. One that puts humans at the center, not data.

Why This Matters Now

Large language models have made this possible in a way that wasn't feasible even two years ago. The combination of semantic understanding and structured data querying opens up entirely new paradigms for how businesses can understand their customers.

We're still early. But the direction is clear. The future of customer analytics isn't more dashboards — it's better conversations.
